Safety Centre

Last updated: 1 March 2026

Safety is foundational to Attune — not an afterthought. We've built anti-fraud and authenticity verification into the core technology. But technology alone isn't enough. Here's everything you need to stay safe on Attune and when meeting people in person.

Staying safe on the platform

Keep personal details private

Don't share your full name, home address, workplace, phone number, or financial information with someone you've just matched with. Take time to build trust before sharing personal details.

Recognise scam patterns

Be cautious of anyone who:

  • Asks for money, gift cards, or cryptocurrency — for any reason.
  • Claims to be abroad or in the military and can't meet in person.
  • Wants to move the conversation off Attune very quickly.
  • Has a story that keeps changing or doesn't add up.
  • Professes strong feelings very early, before you've had a chance to get to know each other.

If something feels off, trust that instinct. Report the account and stop communicating.

Video call before meeting

Before meeting someone in person, have a video call. It confirms they are who they say they are and helps you feel more comfortable.

Meeting in person

First meeting tips

  • Always meet in a public place — a café, restaurant, or busy area. Never at someone's home or a private location for a first meeting.
  • Arrange your own transport to and from the meeting. Don't rely on your date.
  • Tell a friend or family member where you're going, who you're meeting, and when you expect to be back.
  • Keep your phone charged and accessible.
  • Have an exit plan. It's fine to leave if you feel uncomfortable — you don't owe anyone an explanation.

Alcohol and substances

Be mindful of how much you're drinking on a first date. Don't leave your drink unattended and don't accept drinks from someone you don't trust. If you feel unexpectedly unwell, seek help immediately.

Reporting and blocking

You can report any user directly from their profile or from within a conversation. Reports are anonymous. Our safety team will review every report and take appropriate action, which may include warnings, temporary suspension, or permanent removal from the platform.

Blocking a user prevents them from seeing your profile, sending messages, or appearing in your matches. To block someone, tap the three-dot menu on their profile or conversation.

If something has happened to you

If you have experienced sexual assault, harassment, or any crime as a result of meeting someone through Attune:

  • Contact the police if you are in immediate danger or a crime has occurred. In the UK, call 999 (emergency) or 101 (non-emergency).
  • Reach out to Rape Crisis (0808 500 2222) or Victim Support (0808 168 9111) for confidential support.
  • Contact our safety team at safety@attune.love. We will preserve relevant account data and cooperate with law enforcement.

Online harassment and abuse

If you receive threatening, abusive, or sexually explicit messages that you did not consent to, report and block immediately. You can also contact:

  • Online harassment support via Refuge
  • StopNCII — for non-consensual intimate image abuse.
