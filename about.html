The problem

A decade of swiping.
Still the same result.

Modern dating apps were built on the assumption that attraction is visual and compatibility is self-reported. You upload your best photos. You write a carefully crafted bio. You swipe on faces. And then you're surprised when the conversation goes nowhere.

The problem isn't that people are bad at dating. It's that the tools are measuring the wrong thing. A photograph tells you what someone looks like. A questionnaire tells you what someone thinks they want. Neither tells you how someone feels — and feeling is where real connection begins.

We started Attune to fix that. Not with another algorithm that optimises for engagement. With science that actually understands human emotion.

The technology

Emotion AI built by the people who wrote the science.

Attune is an emotion AI dating app that analyses 44 facial Action Units to match users on genuine emotional compatibility — not photos.

Attune is powered by EchoDepth — the emotional intelligence engine developed by Cavefish, a Cardiff-based company specialising in real-time facial Action Unit analysis.

EchoDepth analyses the 44 muscle movements that make up human facial expression — the FACS system originally developed by psychologist Paul Ekman — and maps them to emotional states in real time. No wearables, no sensors, no hardware beyond your phone's front camera.

Your emotional profile is processed entirely on your device. What we store is an anonymised emotional vector: a mathematical fingerprint of how you respond. It cannot be reverse-engineered to reconstruct your face or identify you.
